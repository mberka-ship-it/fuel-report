What changed since yesterday

  • The latest public weekly stock update still points to adequate national supply, but the composition has shifted: petrol cover is stronger than in the earlier March briefing, while diesel and jet-fuel cover are lower.
  • The temporary reduction in the Minimum Stockholding Obligation is active, enabling release of up to 762 million litres of petrol and diesel from domestic stocks.
  • The temporary fuel-quality waiver remains an important Brisbane-specific lever: it allows additional supply from Ampol’s Lytton refinery to stay in the domestic market.
  • Brisbane still has a visible crude-tanker presence on current AIS port pages. That is a supply-positive signal, but it is not a direct measure of local inventory.

4) What should be inferred from inflows?

Yes — with caveats

For a practical domestic fuel-security report, inbound product and crude movements are more useful than national export flows. That said, Australia still exports hydrocarbons such as crude oil / condensate and LNG, so “inflow only” is not literally correct at the national level.

The best operational monitoring stack is therefore:

  1. Official weekly national stock data for petrol, diesel, and jet fuel.
  2. Daily inbound and coastal tanker movements into fuel-relevant ports.
  3. Refinery / terminal signals, especially Lytton for Brisbane.
  4. Retail and wholesale price behavior as a same-day stress indicator.

That gives you a defensible direction-of-travel estimate for Brisbane / Queensland supply tightness even without public daily storage data.

6) Method and caveats

  • Official national stock data is weekly only. Public daily local-inventory data for Brisbane was not identified.
  • The tanker appendix is a proxy dataset. It is best interpreted as a change-detection layer rather than a full harbor-master ledger.
  • AIS sites are informational. Public vessel-tracking platforms explicitly note that their movement data is AIS-derived and not an official record.
  • Best use case: this report is strongest as an internal situational-awareness brief for leaders who need an evidence-based daily snapshot.
  • Best future upgrade: keep the tanker appendix in a maintained dataset so the daily version can compare against a richer multi-week baseline by port and tanker class.
